Privacy Policy

Fleetarr – iOS, iPadOS & macOS app

Fleetarr is a native client that shows a unified status and activity dashboard for a self-hosted media stack you already run (Sonarr, Radarr, Prowlarr, Seerr, SABnzbd, Plex, Jellyfin). It is a monitoring tool: it talks to your own services and keeps its configuration on your own devices. The operator receives essentially no personal data – as detailed below.

1. Controller

The operator of the “Fleetarr” app is Aiko Radlingmayr.
Contact: a.radlingmayr@pm.me

2. What data is processed?

2.1 Service credentials

The API keys, tokens and passwords for your media services are stored only in the device Keychain. Unless you turn iCloud sync off in Settings, they are synchronised across your own Apple devices via iCloud Keychain, which is end-to-end encrypted to your personal trusted-device circle – the same mechanism that protects your Safari passwords. Apple cannot read them, and the operator never receives them. They are used solely to connect to the services you configured and are never transmitted to the operator or any third party. They are never written to logs, analytics, or the synced configuration store.

2.2 Configuration & preferences

Non-secret settings – instance labels, base URLs, service type, enabled state, display order, and dashboard preferences – are stored locally on the device (SwiftData). Unless you turn iCloud sync off, they are synchronised across your own Apple devices via Apple’s CloudKit private database, held inside your personal iCloud account and not accessible to the operator. A Settings toggle disables iCloud sync entirely, keeping everything on the device.

2.3 Usage analytics

The app collects anonymous usage statistics through a self-hosted Aptabase instance on a server operated by the operator, in order to improve the app. This data is not shared with any third party.

  • Aptabase is a cookieless, privacy-friendly analytics service.
  • No user or device identifiers, IP addresses or cookies are stored – the events are anonymous and cannot be linked to a person (no personal data within the meaning of the GDPR, Recital 26).
  • Only event names and coarse technical fields (device model, operating-system and app version, language) are recorded.
  • No personal content is captured – in particular no API keys, media titles, usernames, requester names, server hostnames or URLs, and no free-text error messages; only enum-like categories and counts (e.g. service_type: "sonarr", a problem count).
  • No cookies, advertising identifiers, location data or contact data are collected.
  • No session recordings are created.
  • A Settings toggle disables analytics entirely.

2.4 Network connections

The app connects only to (a) the media services you have configured – to read their status and activity and to carry out the actions you trigger (e.g. approving a request, removing a stuck download, stopping a stream) – and (b) the operator’s self-hosted analytics endpoint. There are no connections to other third parties, no advertising, and no tracking networks.

3. Legal basis

The processing of anonymous usage data is based on the operator’s legitimate interest in improving the app (Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR). Connections to your own media services are your own use of those services, under your control; for the data those services hold, the respective service is the controller.

4. Retention

  • Credentials & configuration: until you delete them in the app or uninstall the app.
  • Analytics: no personal data is stored. Anonymous, aggregated statistics are kept on the operator’s self-hosted Aptabase server.

5. Your rights

The operator itself stores no personal data about you: your credentials stay in your own Keychain / iCloud (end-to-end encrypted) and the usage analytics are anonymous. You can remove all locally stored data at any time by deleting it in the app or uninstalling. Rights regarding data held by your media services (access, rectification, erasure) are exercised directly with those services, which are responsible for them. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with a data-protection supervisory authority.

5.1 Analytics data

Because Aptabase collects and stores no personal data, individual erasure is neither possible nor necessary: there is no analytics ID and no way to attribute stored data to a single person.
